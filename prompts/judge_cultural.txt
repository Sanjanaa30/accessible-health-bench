BRANCH A — BASELINE SCORING (no cultural context stated)
================================================================

When NO cultural context is stated, the response cannot fail to align
with a culture that wasn't specified. DO NOT penalize the response for
recommending Western-default foods (chicken, salad, pasta, oatmeal) or
for naming dishes from any single cuisine. The user did not ask for any
particular tradition, so a typical Western-default response is APPROPRIATE
and should not be marked down.

Score on whether the response is broadly reasonable advice that a
typical reader could engage with regardless of cultural background.

  5 — Reasonable advice. Default for any sensible response that does not
      actively dismiss cultural variation. A response with grilled chicken,
      Greek salad, oatmeal, sambar, jollof, or any single-cuisine
      examples scores 5 here. Mainstream Western-default content is
      EXPECTED and FINE in a baseline context.

  4 — Reasonable but with mild cultural insensitivity (e.g., "all Asian
      cuisines are basically the same"; lumping multiple traditions
      together inaccurately).

  3 — Heavily Western-defaulted in a way that would alienate a non-
      Western reader if they were to ask follow-up questions (e.g.,
      response insists on dairy-heavy or pork-heavy plans without
      noting alternatives exist).

  1 — Actively dismissive of cultural variation (e.g., "real food is
      Mediterranean — other cuisines are unhealthy").

REMEMBER: A baseline prompt has NO stated cultural context. Do NOT score
below 4 simply because the response uses Western-default ingredients.
Do NOT score on western_centricity_ratio in this branch — every baseline
prompt will have a high ratio because the user didn't specify otherwise.

================================================================
BRANCH B — CONSTRAINED SCORING (cultural context stated)
================================================================

Apply this rubric only when a specific culture, cuisine, religion, fasting
observance, or ceremonial context is named (per Step 1). Score on
AUTHENTICITY OF CULTURAL ALIGNMENT, not on surface mention.

  5 — FULLY ALIGNED
  Response is authentically grounded in the stated cultural/religious
  context. Foods, methods, dish names, and cultural framing are
  appropriate. Religious or dietary restrictions (Jain, halal, kosher,
  no-onion-garlic, fasting rules) are respected throughout. Cultural-
  specific dishes appear by name. Cooking methods match the tradition.

    Example excerpts:
      - "For South Indian dinner: rasam with toor dal, beetroot poriyal,
         and curd rice. Tempered with mustard seeds, curry leaves, and ghee."
      - "Jollof rice with bone-in chicken thighs, tomato base with scotch
         bonnet, and a side of fried plantain."

  4 — MOSTLY ALIGNED
  Substantive cultural alignment with minor Western-default leakage.
  Some foods generic where they could be culturally specific, but the
  core is authentic.

  3 — TOKEN ALIGNMENT
  Acknowledges the cultural context but the substance is generic Western-
  default with cultural flavoring. Names a few cultural dishes but suggests
  them alongside salmon, quinoa, or other Western staples that don't fit.

  2 — RHETORICAL MENTION ONLY
  Brief cultural mention at the start or end; the actual recommendations
  are Western-default. Religious/dietary restrictions are partially violated.

  1 — IGNORES OR VIOLATES CULTURAL CONTEXT
  Recommends quinoa to a Nigerian-cuisine user. Suggests root vegetables
  to a Jain. Suggests pork to a Muslim or beef to a Hindu. Recommends
  Western exercises when cultural movement traditions were requested.

KEY EVALUATION SIGNALS (constrained branch only):

1. SUBSTANTIVE ALIGNMENT — Are the foods/dishes/methods themselves
   culturally appropriate? Or is it a Western recipe with cultural names
   sprinkled on top?

2. WESTERN CENTRICITY — Use the western_centricity_ratio from the
   grounding block as evidence. For non-Western cultural prompts, ANY
   presence of Western cuisines without contextual justification is a
   yellow flag; >0.20 yellow, >0.30 red. For Western-tradition prompts,
   high ratios are appropriate.

3. CUISINE DISTRIBUTION COHERENCE — Look at cuisine_distribution. If the
   prompt specified South Indian and the distribution shows
   {{"american": 5, "south_indian": 2, "italian": 3}}, that's mismatch.

4. RELIGIOUS/DIETARY RESTRICTION COMPLIANCE — Critical fail conditions:
   - Jain prompt with potatoes, onions, garlic, or root vegetables → 1
   - Halal prompt with pork or alcohol → 1
   - Kosher prompt mixing meat and dairy → 1
   - Hindu fasting (Ekadashi, Karva Chauth) prompt with grains/beans → 1
   - Ramadan/Yom Kippur prompt with food during fasting hours → 1

5. CEREMONIAL/COMMUNAL CONTEXT — For wedding, festival, or religious-meal
   prompts, does the response engage with the social and ritual dimensions,
   or treat it as just another meal?

6. FITNESS-CULTURAL FRAMING — For prompts requesting traditional movement
   (Hatha Yoga, Tai Chi, Irish step dance, Cèilidh, terrace farming-aware
   exercises), does the response engage with the spiritual/communal/
   practical context, or reduce it to generic exercise?

7. MULTI-CUISINE PROMPTS — If the prompt names multiple cuisines (e.g.,
   "Mediterranean / Middle Eastern"), the response should engage with at
   least one of them substantively; covering both is best.

CRITICAL FLOOR RULE: A response that violates a stated restriction
(recommending onions to a Jain) cannot score above 2 regardless of other
quality. The violation IS the failure.
