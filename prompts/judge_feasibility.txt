Stated constraints (machine-readable, may be empty):

Model response:

{response_text}

Grounding evidence (Phase 4 enrichment — Compendium MET values for matched

{grounding_block_json}

================================================================
TASK
================================================================
Score LIFESTYLE FEASIBILITY on a 1–5 scale. Score on REAL EXECUTABILITY,
not on rhetorical engagement.

================================================================
SCORING RUBRIC (1–5)
================================================================

5 — FULLY EXECUTABLE
The plan fits comfortably within the stated time/equipment/space/mobility
constraints. Time totals are realistic (a "20-minute breakfast" actually
takes 20 minutes including cleanup). Equipment requirements match what the
user has. Plan structure genuinely accommodates the constraint (batch
cooking for time-poor users; no-cook options for no-stove users; chair-
based exercises for mobility-limited users). For unconstrained baselines,
score 5 if reasonable for a typical adult.

  Example excerpts:
    - "Each breakfast takes 5 minutes the night before; grab-and-go in
       the morning."
    - "Bodyweight circuit, 20 minutes, no equipment, fits in 6×6 ft."

4 — MOSTLY EXECUTABLE
Plan is largely feasible with minor friction. One or two items push the
time/equipment limit slightly. The constraint shaped the plan, even if
imperfectly.

  Example excerpts:
    - "Most meals are 20 minutes, except Saturday's stew which is 45."

3 — BORDERLINE
Plan acknowledges the constraint but the substance demands more than the
user can give. Total time exceeds the stated limit by 25–50%. Some
equipment is assumed that the user said they don't have. The plan is the
model's standard template with constraint-flavored garnishes.

  Example excerpts:
    - "These are quick weeknight meals." (when 4 of 5 take 45+ minutes)

2 — PARTIALLY UNREALISTIC
Plan demands substantially more than the user can give. Recommends 60+
minutes of cooking to someone with 20 minutes. Assumes a full kitchen for
someone in a hotel. Recommends a gym to someone who said no gym.

  Example excerpts:
    - "Roast a whole chicken on Sunday for the week's protein"
       (when prompt said "I only have a microwave")

1 — IGNORES OR VIOLATES CONSTRAINTS
Recommends 2-hour gym sessions to someone working 12-hour shifts. Assumes
full kitchen for someone with no electricity. Recommends running to someone
who specified mobility limitations. The constraint is treated as if it
weren't there.

  Example excerpts:
    - "Hit the gym for 90 minutes after work" (when prompt said
       "no money for a gym membership and I work two jobs")

================================================================
KEY EVALUATION SIGNALS
================================================================

1. TIME REALISM — Look at extracted prep_time_minutes and the Compendium
   total_logged_minutes. Does the plan's total time fit the stated schedule?
   Concrete anchors:
   - If extracted_max_prep > stated_limit × 1.5  → score ≤ 2 on time-fit
   - If extracted_max_prep > stated_limit × 1.25 → score ≤ 3 on time-fit
   - If within 1.0–1.25× → fits

2. EQUIPMENT MATCH — Look at feasibility_signals.kitchen_access_assumption
   and fitness_equipment_required vs. what the prompt allowed:
   - Hotel microwave / dorm stated → only microwave-based or no-cook recipes
   - No electricity → only no-cook recipes
   - No gym stated → only home/bodyweight exercises
   - Small space stated → no exercises that need 6+ ft of clearance

3. STRUCTURAL ADAPTATION — Critical and often missed. Did the plan
   ACTUALLY restructure to fit the constraint, or is it a standard plan
   with the constraint mentioned as a footnote?
   - Standard plan + "you can adapt this for your busy schedule" → low
   - Plan built around 5-min meals, batch prep, single-pot recipes → high

4. WHO COMPLIANCE — For fitness plans with weekly time horizon, check
   moderate_equivalent_minutes against WHO 2020 (150 min moderate or 75
   min vigorous per week).
   - If the user said they have very limited time AND the plan stays
     within the stated time, do NOT penalize for under-prescribing —
     the plan is feasible by definition.
   - If the plan exceeds the stated time, penalize for infeasibility.
   - If the response specifies fitness components but no time horizon,
     assume weekly cadence and score WHO compliance with that assumption
     flagged in reasoning.

5. MOBILITY/HEALTH SENSITIVITY — For prompts mentioning chronic fatigue,
   burnout, recovery, mobility limits, or disability, score harshly if
   the response treats the user as a typical able-bodied adult.

6. ENVIRONMENTAL CONSTRAINT — For prompts with environmental constraints
   (air pollution / mega-city / food desert / extreme climate), does the
   response engage with these or ignore them?

================================================================
SPECIAL CASES
================================================================

- BASELINE PROMPTS (no lifestyle constraint stated): Score 5 if reasonable
  for a typical adult with average resources. Score 3 if the plan
  secretly demands extreme time or expensive equipment without warning.
  Score 1 only if absurd (e.g., recommends 4 hours/day of training to a
  generic fitness question).

- NUTRITION-ONLY RESPONSES (no fitness content): Evaluate on kitchen,
  time, equipment, mobility for cooking and eating. Don't penalize the
  absence of fitness components.

- FITNESS-ONLY RESPONSES (no nutrition content): Evaluate on time,
  equipment, mobility, and space for the exercises. Don't penalize the
  absence of meal plans.

- SHARED KITCHEN / ROOMMATE / DORM CONTEXT: Penalize plans that require
  occupying the kitchen for extended periods or that require equipment
  the user is unlikely to own in shared housing.

================================================================
